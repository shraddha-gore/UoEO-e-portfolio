e-Portfolio Activity: Research Proposal Review

Considering your thoughts on your chosen area of interest for your project, respond to the questions below.

Note that you may find that you could be using a mixture of both the research methods and the data collection methods. These considerations will be included in your presentation of the Project Proposal in unit 10.

1. Which of the methods described in this week's reading would you think would suit your purpose?

At this early stage in the module, I am inclined towards adopting a qualitative research approach, as this would enable an in-depth exploration of how e-learning can be implemented more effectively for working professionals. My current thinking aligns with an exploratory research design, as the aim is to investigate existing practices, challenges, and opportunities rather than to test a specific hypothesis. However, as I progress through the later units, I may choose to adapt, combine, or even replace this initial methodological choice based on my developing understanding.

2. Which data collection methods would you consider using?

Although qualitative research frequently involves primary data collection methods such as interviews or focus groups, I intend, at least for this module, to focus exclusively on secondary data sources. These will include peer-reviewed journal articles, published surveys, reputable online materials, books and industry reports. This approach will allow me to build a robust evidence base without engaging in direct primary research. I recognise that my approach to data collection may evolve in later stages, and I remain open to integrating additional methods should they strengthen the project.

3. Which required skills will you need to have or develop for the chosen project??

The project will require skills in advanced literature searching, critical evaluation of academic and industry sources, thematic analysis to identify patterns and themes, and coherent academic writing using the Harvard referencing style. As I am still developing my methodological confidence, strengthening these skills will be essential to ensure the quality and credibility of my work. One certainty is my chosen topic, Implementation of e-learning, which resonates strongly with my own experience as an online MSc Computer Science student and enables me to contribute both academically and from personal insight.

Reflection

  • The content in this unit was instrumental in helping me understand the research proposal. I complemented the lecture material by watching several videos and doing additional reading to gain confidence. I have since decided to refine my topic, narrowing the focus to 'The Implementation of E-Learning for Working Professionals'. This has been an iterative process, but it has helped clarify my direction.
